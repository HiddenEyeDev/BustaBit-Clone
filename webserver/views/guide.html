Strategy Guide

A practical guide to understanding Bustabit’s mechanics and maximizing your chances.

Bustabit is a game of 99% luck and 1% skill. That means if you play without a strategy, you won’t perform terribly — but with a sharp plan, it’s possible to edge out the house and even turn a profit.

Here’s how it works:

Instabust

Every round has a 1% chance of instantly busting. When that happens, no one can win — this creates the house edge. The expected return to the house is:

1% × playersInGame × tableAmount

That’s the **exact amount** Bustabit uses to fund its bonus pool.

House Edge

The house edge scales between 0% and 1% — see: odds and calculator. This is the barrier you need to overcome to become profitable.

Bonuses

The last person to cash out before a bust earns the round’s bonus, which on average equals the house’s instabust profit. This means the key to strategic play is timing your cash-out perfectly.

If no one wins the bonus, it rolls over to the next game — see FAQ.

Single Player Game

If you play alone, there’s no one else to compete for bonuses — meaning you can’t overcome the house odds. In this case, the game becomes pure luck.

Simple Greedy Strategy

Stay in until you’re the last player holding — then cash out immediately. This works great if your opponents are cautious and cash out early.

But beware — if two players use the same greedy approach, you’ll both keep holding indefinitely and never win.
